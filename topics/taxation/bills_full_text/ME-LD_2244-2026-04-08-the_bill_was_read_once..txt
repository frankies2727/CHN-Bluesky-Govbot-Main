Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:

Sec. 1. 36 MRSA §5219-KK, sub-§2-D, as amended by PL 2023, c. 412, Pt. S,

§4, is repealed and the following enacted in its place:

2-D. Credit in 2022 and after. For tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2022,
a resident individual is allowed a credit against the taxes imposed under this Part equal to
the amount by which the benefit base for the resident individual exceeds 4% of the resident
individual's income. The amount of the credit may not exceed:

A. For tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2022 and before January 1, 2026,
$1,000 for resident individuals under 65 years of age as of the last day of the taxable
year;

B. For tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2026, $1,500 for resident individuals
under 65 years of age as of the last day of the taxable year;

C. For tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2022 and before January 1, 2024,
$1,500 for resident individuals 65 years of age and older as of the last day of the taxable
year; and

D. For tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2024, $2,000 for resident individuals
years of age and older as of the last day of the taxable year.

In the case of married individuals filing a joint return, only one spouse is required to be 65
years of age or older to qualify for the credit limitation specified in paragraphs C and D.
Married individuals filing separate returns do not qualify for the credit under this section.

Sec. 2. Resolve 2025, c. 108, §5 is amended to read:

Sec. 5. Meetings; subcommittees. Resolved: That the task force shall meet at
least 4 times but no more than 8 times in 2025 and no more than 8 times in 2026, not
including any subcommittee meetings.

1. The task force shall meet at least once jointly with the Joint Standing Committee on
Taxation at a time to be determined by the chairs of the task force and the committee.

2. The chairs of the task force may appoint subcommittees as necessary for the efficient
operation of the task force.

Sec. 3. Department of Administrative and Financial Services, Bureau of

Law. The Department of Administrative and Financial Services, Bureau of Revenue

The Bureau of Revenue Services shall submit its findings and recommendations based

joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over taxation matters. The

Services shall administer a survey to assessors related to the Maine Tree Growth Tax Law.

SUMMARY

This bill amends Resolve 2025, chapter 108, which established the Real Estate Property
Tax Relief Task Force, to allow the task force to meet 8 times in 2026. For tax years
beginning on or after January 1, 2026, the bill increases from $1,000 to $1,500 the property
tax fairness credit for individuals under 65 years of age. The bill also requires the
Department of Administrative and Financial Services, Bureau of Revenue Services to
administer a survey to assessors to gather data on the administration of the Maine Tree
Growth Tax Law.
