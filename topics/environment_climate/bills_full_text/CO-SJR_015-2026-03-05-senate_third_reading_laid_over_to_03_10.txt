SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 26-015

CONCERNING THE PROTECTION OF THE PUBLIC'S INTEREST IN THE

MANAGEMENT OF COLORADO'S NATIONAL PUBLIC LANDS.

WHEREAS, Coloradans have a deep, unifying love and affection
for our shared national public lands and the essential, diverse benefits
they provide to everyone; and

WHEREAS, Our national public lands, including national parks,
forests, monuments, national wildlife refuges, and Bureau of Land
Management lands, are foundational to Coloradans' quality of life,
economy, and common heritage, offering invaluable recreational,
economic, cultural, and spiritual opportunities; and

WHEREAS, Outdoor recreation, intrinsically linked to our public
lands, contributed over $36.5 billion to Colorado's economy in 2023,
supported more than 404,000 jobs, and demonstrated the profound
economic value of these resources when managed for the public good;
and

WHEREAS, According to the 2024 Colorado College State of the
Rockies poll, 88 percent of Westerners visited national public lands in the

percent of Coloradans support the creation of new national parks, national
monuments, and national wildlife refuges; and

WHEREAS, Our public lands provide important wildlife habitats,
clean water, clean air, and natural amenities that contribute to Coloradans'
quality of life and Colorado's climate resiliency; and

WHEREAS, In 2016, through Senate Bill 16-021, Colorado
created a state holiday to recognize and pay tribute to our public lands:
Public Lands Day on the third Saturday of each May; and

WHEREAS, The effective stewardship of these lands requires a
robust partnership between the federal government and the American
public, ensuring decisions reflect the diverse interests and values of all
citizens, regardless of location or background; and

WHEREAS, Bedrock federal laws such as the "Federal Land
Policy and Management Act of 1976" (FLPMA) and the "National
Environmental Policy Act of 1969" (NEPA) were enacted to define the
federal government's fiduciary duty to the American people, requiring
balanced resource management, ensuring that federal decisions are
informed by public input, and protecting the long-term, sustainable use
of public lands; and

WHEREAS, Recent actions at the federal level, including
significant policy changes, the reduction of on-the-ground management
resources, and the streamlining of regulatory processes, risk undermining
the bedrock principles of effective public land stewardship; and

WHEREAS, While not an overt sell-off, these cumulative actions,
such as diminishing public engagement opportunities, shortening review
timelines, and increasing regulatory instability, have the troubling effect
of centralizing power in Washington, D.C., while marginalizing the
voices of local communities and land users; and

WHEREAS, This shift toward a less accountable management
structure reduces transparency and legal durability, disproportionately
favoring certain expedited private interests over the long-term, balanced
needs of the public, thereby creating a de facto loss of the public's access,
involvement, and recourse regarding the future of their shared national
assets; and

WHEREAS, Protecting the integrity of the public process and
ensuring fair access to our shared national resources are fundamentally
nonpartisan issues that uphold the democratic ideals embodied by our
public land system; now, therefore,

Be It Resolved by the Senate of the Seventy-fifth General Assembly
of the State of Colorado, the House of Representatives concurring herein:

That we, the members of the Colorado General Assembly:

(1) Declare our unwavering support for maintaining our national
public lands under the stewardship of the United States government and
the federal land management agencies, and for the foundational public
processes designed to guide their sustainable use;

(2) Oppose all efforts, whether legislative or administrative, that
directly or indirectly diminish the public's voice, access, and recourse in
the management of national public lands, including the erosion of
bedrock laws such as the "National Environmental Policy Act of 1969"
(NEPA) and the "Federal Land Policy and Management Act of 1976"
(FLPMA); and

(3) Call upon the members of Colorado's congressional
delegation, the governor, and the attorney general to take all necessary
actions to defend the public process and statutory safeguards that ensure
public lands are managed for the broadest possible public benefit, rather
than being effectively privatized or subjugated to narrow special interests.

Be It Further Resolved, That copies of this Joint Resolution be sent
to Governor Jared Polis; United States Senators John Hickenlooper and
Michael Bennet; United States Representatives Diana DeGette, Joe
Neguse, Jeff Hurd, Lauren Boebert, Jeff Crank, Jason Crow, Brittany
Pettersen, and Gabe Evans; Secretary of the Interior Doug Burgum; and
President Donald J. Trump.
